Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about SmileBright Dental

How do I schedule an appointment? +

You can schedule an appointment by:

  • Calling us at (555) 123-4567
  • Using our online booking form on the Appointment page
  • Emailing us at appointments@smilebright.com

We recommend booking 2-3 weeks in advance for routine cleanings, but we offer same-day appointments for emergencies.

Do you accept insurance? +

Yes! We accept most major dental insurance plans including:

  • Delta Dental
  • Cigna
  • Aetna
  • MetLife
  • Guardian

We also offer flexible payment plans and financing options through CareCredit. Call us to verify your specific plan.

What should I bring to my first visit? +

Please bring:

  • Valid ID
  • Insurance card (if applicable)
  • List of current medications
  • Any recent dental X-rays (if available)
  • Completed new patient forms (we'll email these to you)
Is dental treatment painful? +

We prioritize your comfort! We use:

  • Advanced local anesthetics for numbing
  • Laser dentistry for minimally invasive procedures
  • Sedation options for anxious patients
  • TV and music during treatments

Most patients report little to no discomfort during their procedures.

At what age should my child first see a dentist? +

The American Dental Association recommends children have their first dental visit by age 1 or within 6 months of their first tooth appearing. Early visits help:

  • Prevent cavities
  • Build positive dental habits
  • Catch developmental issues early
Do you offer payment plans? +

Yes! We offer:

  • CareCredit medical financing (6-12 months interest-free)
  • In-house payment plans for larger treatments
  • 10% discount for upfront cash payments
  • Senior and military discounts

Contact our billing department to discuss options that fit your budget.

How often should I get dental cleanings? +

Most patients should get professional cleanings every 6 months. However, some patients may need more frequent visits (every 3-4 months) if they have:

  • Gum disease
  • Diabetes
  • A history of frequent cavities
  • Smoking habits
